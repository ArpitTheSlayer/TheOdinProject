Onion Pakoda

Description

Grilled cheese sandwich is one of our favorite quick and simple vegetarian recipes that only requires a few key ingredients. Its made with a good loaf of bread (fresh or day old) a tangy block of cheddar or mozzarella cheese, a bit of softened butter, and flavorful spices and herbs.

Ingredients

  • 2 onions medium to large-sized
  • 1 cup gram flour (besan) or chickpea flour
  • 1 to 2 teaspoon green chillies about ½ to 1 teaspoon chopped green chillies or swap with ½ teaspoon red chilli powder or cayenne pepper
  • 1 tablespoon chopped coriander leaves (cilantro) optional
  • ½ teaspoon Garam Masala optional
  • ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder optional
  • 1 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1 generous pinch asafoetida (hing) optional or use gluten-free asafoetida
  • 1 pinch baking soda optional
  • oil as required for shallow frying or deep frying, sunflower oil or any neutral-flavoured oil
  • water as required to make a medium thick batter
  • salt as required

Steps

  1. Slice the onions thinly and take them in a mixing bowl. Also, add chopped green chillies.
  2. If you do not have green chillies, then add red chilli powder. You can also add chopped coriander leaves if you want.
  3. Add the spices carom seeds, turmeric powder, asafoetida and salt.
  4. Mix everything well. Cover and keep the onion, chillies and spice mixture aside for 15 to 20 minutes.
  5. The onions would release water and then you can add water as required in the batter.
  6. Add gram flour (besan). If you plan to add baking soda, then add at this step.
  7. Add the required amount of water to make medium-thick batter.
  8. Stir the whole mixture very well with a spoon or with your hands. The batter is ready to be fried.
  9. In hot oil, add spoonfuls of the batter.
  10. Depending on the size of the kadai or pan, you can add less or more. Just make sure you do not over crowd the onion pakoda while frying.
  11. When the pakora are a bit cooked, turn over with a slotted spoon and continue to fry.
  12. You will have to turn them a few times for even frying.
  13. Fry them till they look crisp and golden.
  14. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on kitchen paper towels for excess oil to be absorbed.
  15. In the same oil fry slit green chilies.
  16. Sprinkle some salt on the green chilies and mix well.
  17. Serve onion pakoda with the fried green chilies or coriander chutney or tomato sauce.
